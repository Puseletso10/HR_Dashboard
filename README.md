# 📊 Human Resources Dashboard

## 🧾 Project Overview
This project focuses on building an interactive HR dashboard to help Human Resources teams easily access and analyze employee data.

The dashboard provides insights into employee distribution, demographics, performance, and salary trends, enabling data-driven decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
The dashboard answers key HR questions such as:
- How many employees were hired, are active, and terminated?
- What is the distribution of employees across departments and locations?
- What is the gender distribution of employees?
- How do education levels relate to performance?
- What are the salary trends across roles and demographics?

---

## 🛠 Tools Used
- Tableau (Data visualization)
- CSV datasets

---

## 📌 Key Features
- Interactive filters (gender, status, location, hire date)
- Department and role analysis
- Employee demographic insights (age, gender, education)
- Salary analysis by role and gender
- Drill-down functionality to view individual employee details
- Export options (PDF/image)

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 👥 Workforce Overview
- Total employees hired: **8,950**
- Active employees: **7,985**
- Terminated employees: **966**

---

### 🏢 Departments
- Operations has the highest number of employees  
- HR has the lowest number of employees  

---

### 📍 Location
- 70% of employees work at headquarters  
- 30% are in branches  
- New York has the highest number of employees  

---

### ⚖️ Gender Distribution
- 54% Male, 46% Female  
- 89% of employees are active  

---

### 🎓 Education & Performance
- Higher education levels generally correlate with better performance  
- Employees with PhDs have the highest percentage of “Excellent” ratings  

---

### 💰 Salary Insights
- Finance Managers have the highest average salary (~125K)  
- HR Assistants have the lowest average salary (~60K)
